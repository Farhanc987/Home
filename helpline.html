HELP LINE

What can we do

This page is to help young minds contact lines they feel they may need to contact, 50% of mental health problems are established by age 14 and 75% by age 24. 10% of children and young people (aged 5-16 years) have a clinically diagnosable mental problem, yet 70% of children and adolescents who experience mental health problems have not had appropriate interventions at a sufficiently early age. It is in our best interest to ensure that you have readily available at any time if needed, help us and help yourself dont let yourself become a statistic.

Numbers 4 You

We provide 24/7 support to anyone in need, having someone to talk to can improve mental state for those who may not feel comfortable to speak to those around them, its important to release the built up tension by talking about your experiences to people that can support, and thats why we've provided the following numbers below

National Suicide Prevention Helpline UK. - 0800 689 5652

The National Suicide Prevention Helpline UK. Offers a supportive listening service to anyone with thoughts of suicide. You can call the National Suicide Prevention Helpline UK on the number above (open 24/7). - 24/7 Including Christmas Day

Samaritans - 0808 164 0123

To talk about anything that is upsetting you, you can contact Samaritans 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. You can call 116 123 (free from any phone),ou can also call the Samaritans Welsh Language Line on above (7pm–11pm every day).

SANEline - 0300 304 7000

SANEline is a national out-of-hours mental health helpline offering specialist emotional support, guidance and information to anyone affected by mental illness, including family, friends and carers. Open every day of the year from 4pm to 10pm on the number above

The Mix - 0808 808 4994

The Mix is the UK’s leading support service for young people. We are here to help you take on any challenge you’re facing - from mental health to money, from homelessness to finding a job, from break-ups to drugs. Talk to us via our online community, on social, through our free, confidential helpline or our counselling service

Anxiety UK - 03444 775 774

We offer support, advice and information on all anxiety, stress and anxiety-based depression conditions. Our helpline services are open to anyone of any age, (parental consent is not required to call the helpline and you can read more about confidentiality below), with an interpretation service available to those who require it.

NAMI - 800-950-6264

Coping with BPD can be very challenging. But if you, a family member or friend is struggling, there is help. NAMI is here to provide you with support and information about community resources for you and your family.
